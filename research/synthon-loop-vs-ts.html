Same brain, different hands

Both loops are Thompson Sampling: keep a belief for each piece, sample, act, update. The only real difference is step 3 — true TS builds the molecule it picked; we can't build, so we steer a similarity window and decompose whatever comes back.

⟲Repeat until the top hits stop improving. Both loops concentrate effort on the pieces that score well.

Because we decompose every docked analog, the learning half now matches true TS — exact per-synthon credit. What stays different is the acting half: TS constructs any molecule it wants from the catalog; we can only nudge onepot's similarity window and take what it returns — so our reach is a neighborhood of the seed, not the whole library.
